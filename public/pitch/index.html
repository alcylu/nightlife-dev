Let me walk you through the unit economics driving each stream to $83,000 MRR at month 12. Every number is bottom-up — customers times price — never top-down market capture.

Hotel Concierge App: This is our bankable near-term revenue. We start with Tokyo hotel partnerships at $299-599 per month, growing from $5,000 in month 1 to $19,000 at month 12 across 45 properties at a $422 average. This stream doesn't hockey-stick — it grows steadily to $28,000 by Q4Y2 and $38,000 by Q4Y3. That's the point: it's reliable, provable revenue that de-risks the business while we build the higher-leverage streams.

API/MCP Licensing: This is the AEO thesis — small now, dominant later. Just $500 in month 1 from early developer access, growing to $8,000 at M12 across 35 subscribers at $229 average. The real inflection hits at month 18 as AI agent adoption accelerates — by Q4Y2 we're at $48,000, and by Q4Y3 API becomes our largest stream at $195,000 per month. The $99-499 tiered pricing captures both indie developers and enterprise hotel chains.

VIP Commissions: Our highest-conviction stream — live venues, live demand, live revenue from day one. 10 venues with 11 bookings per venue at $255 average commission drives $28,000 at month 12. Linear growth with city launches takes this to $52,000 at Q4Y2 and $90,000 by Q4Y3. Commission revenue runs at 20-30% net margin after payment processing.

Guest List SaaS: Follows the VIP pipeline from month 5 — 50 venues at $240 average by M12 for $12,000 per month. Pure SaaS margin following the SevenRooms model. Grows to $30,000 at Q4Y2 and $58,000 by Q4Y3.

Promoted Listings: 47 advertisers at $234 average by M12 for $11,000 per month. Slow and steady — $22,000 at Q4Y2, $38,000 by Q4Y3. Like Google Ads, compounds naturally with traffic and city count. Margins 75%+.

Ticketing: Our sleeper stream — launches month 10 at $2,000, modest at $5,000 by M12 with 1,100 tickets at 12% take rate. But the 18-month bump coincides with API ecosystem maturity: $20,000 at Q4Y2, then accelerating to $95,000 by Q4Y3 as AI-driven event distribution matures alongside the API network.

The revenue mix tells a deliberate story: Hotel Concierge provides bankable base revenue while VIP commissions prove market demand — together they're the near-term engine. API/MCP Licensing and Ticketing are the long-term bets that inflect together around month 18. Total at month 12: $83,000 MRR across 187 paying relationships, with 60%+ blended gross margin trending toward 70% as SaaS share grows.
